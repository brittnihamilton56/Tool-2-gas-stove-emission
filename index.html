Cabin Kitchen PM2.5 Design Tool

Compare two engineering alternatives for reducing indoor PM2.5 from cooking in a small cabin kitchen: (1) ventilation upgrade and (2) induction stove conversion.

User Inputs

This tool hides raw emission-rate inputs and instead uses built-in assumptions based on cooking intensity. That makes it easier to use while still keeping the assumptions visible below.

Model + Assumptions

This tool uses a simplified single-zone indoor air model.

dC/dt = E/V − k(C − Cbg)
  • C = indoor PM2.5 concentration
  • E = cooking emission rate
  • V = room volume
  • k = total removal rate
  • Cbg = background PM2.5

Assumptions: the room is well mixed, emissions are constant during cooking, and ventilation plus deposition remove particles. The ventilation alternative increases removal; the induction alternative reduces emissions at the source.
