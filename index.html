Model + Assumptions

Single-zone indoor air model.

Model

dC/dt = E/V − k(C − Cbg)
  • C = indoor PM2.5 concentration
  • E = cooking emission rate
  • V = room volume
  • k = total removal rate
  • Cbg = background PM2.5 concentration

Key assumptions

  • The kitchen behaves as a single well-mixed indoor space.
  • Cooking emissions remain constant during the selected cooking period.
  • Ventilation is represented using ACH, meaning air changes per hour.
  • ACH describes how many times the air in the room is replaced in one hour.
  • A breathing rate is used to estimate inhaled PM2.5 during and after cooking.
  • The ventilation alternative increases particle removal.
  • The induction alternative reduces emissions at the source.

How scoring works

Technical effectiveness comes from the modeled PM2.5 reduction. Cost and practicality respond to installation cost and retrofit complexity. Public health and environmental scores reward stronger source control and lower exposure.

Why the recommendation can change

The best overall result depends on the user priority, room size, cooking conditions, and whether induction would require electrical upgrades. This makes the comparison more realistic than assuming one option is always best.
