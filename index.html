Cabin Kitchen PM2.5 Design Tool

Compare two engineering alternatives for reducing indoor PM2.5 from cooking in a small cabin kitchen: ventilation upgrade versus induction stove conversion.

User Inputs

Simple scenario-based inputs instead of raw engineering parameters.

This version keeps the tool easier to use by hiding raw emission-rate inputs and using built-in assumptions based on cooking intensity.

Model + Assumptions

A simplified single-zone indoor air model.

Well-mixed room Constant cooking emissions Ventilation + deposition removal
dC/dt = E/V − k(C − Cbg)
  • C = indoor PM2.5 concentration
  • E = cooking emission rate
  • V = room volume
  • k = total removal rate
  • Cbg = background PM2.5

The ventilation alternative increases particle removal. The induction alternative reduces emissions at the source.
